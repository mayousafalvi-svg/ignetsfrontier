Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or interact with our services related to Frontier, CenturyLink, and Lumen dedicated internet procurement.

By using this website, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with the terms, please discontinue use of the site and services.

Last updated: December 24, 2025

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how we handle personal information in connection with our website and services that support business fiber and dedicated internet access procurement. It applies to visitors, prospective customers, and existing customers who interact with us online or offline.

For purposes of this Privacy Policy, “we,” “us,” or “our” refers to the dedicated internet procurement practice operating this website, and “you” refers to any individual accessing or using our website or services on their own behalf or on behalf of a business entity.

We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your information in a responsible, transparent manner and in accordance with applicable data protection laws where we operate.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect information about you in various ways when you use our website, request quotes, or otherwise communicate with us. The types of information we may collect include:

2.1 Information you provide to us

When you contact us, request a dedicated internet quote, submit a form, or communicate with us by email or phone, you may provide:

  • Contact details such as your name, business email address, phone number, and job title.
  • Company details such as business name, billing details, and locations where you require connectivity.
  • Project information such as bandwidth requirements, preferred carriers, installation timelines, and technical constraints.
  • Any other information you choose to provide in free-text fields or during conversations with our team.

2.2 Information collected automatically

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect certain information about your device and how you interact with the site. This may include:

  • Device and browser information (e.g., IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers).
  • Usage data (e.g., pages visited, time spent on pages, referring/exit pages, clickstream data).
  • Approximate location information derived from your IP address.

We use this information primarily to understand and improve website performance, maintain security, and ensure a consistent user experience.

2.3 Information from third parties

We may receive information about you from third-party sources, such as:

  • Connectivity providers (e.g., Frontier, CenturyLink, Lumen, and other carriers) in connection with quote requests, service availability, and service delivery.
  • Business partners, channel partners, or referral sources who share your information with us so we can follow up on connectivity requirements.
  • Publicly available sources, such as business registries or professional networking sites, where permitted by law.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for a variety of legitimate business purposes, including to:

  • Provide, operate, and maintain our website and services.
  • Respond to your inquiries, quote requests, and support needs.
  • Design, source, and procure dedicated internet and business fiber solutions tailored to your requirements.
  • Communicate with you about proposals, contracts, service delivery milestones, and service-related updates.
  • Manage customer relationships, including billing and account administration where applicable.
  • Analyze how our website and services are used to improve functionality, performance, and user experience.
  • Protect the security and integrity of our systems, detect and prevent fraud or abuse, and comply with legal obligations.
  • Send you relevant information about our services, events, or insights, where permitted by law and subject to your communication preferences.

3.1 Legal bases for processing (where applicable)

Depending on your jurisdiction, our legal bases for processing your personal information may include:

  • The performance of a contract or to take steps at your request prior to entering into a contract.
  • Our legitimate business interests, such as improving services, maintaining security, and managing customer relationships.
  • Your consent, where required for specific activities (e.g., certain types of marketing communications).
  • Compliance with legal obligations and the establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.

4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

Our website may use cookies and similar tracking technologies to collect and store certain information when you visit or interact with the site.

4.1 What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your web browser. They are widely used to make websites work, remember preferences, and provide aggregated analytics about site usage.

4.2 Types of cookies we may use

  • Essential cookies: Necessary for the website to function properly and to enable features such as page navigation and access to secure areas.
  • Performance and analytics cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our site, so we can measure and improve its performance.
  • Functional cookies: Allow the website to remember your preferences (such as language or region) and provide enhanced features.

4.3 Managing cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings, including blocking or deleting cookies. If you choose to disable cookies, some parts of our website may not function properly or may be less tailored to your needs.

To learn more about cookies, including how to see what cookies have been set and how to manage or delete them, you can visit independent resources such as www.allaboutcookies.org or the help section of your browser.

5. Sharing and Disclosure of Information

We may share your information with third parties in the following circumstances:

  • Service providers and partners: We may share information with carriers (such as Frontier, CenturyLink, Lumen, and other providers), consultants, and service providers who assist in designing, quoting, delivering, and supporting connectivity solutions.
  • Business transfers: If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, restructuring, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to appropriate confidentiality protections.
  • Legal and compliance: We may disclose information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities, or to protect our rights, privacy, safety, or property, and that of our customers or the public.
  • With your consent: We may share information with third parties when you have given us your consent to do so or when you direct us to share it as part of a procurement or partnership process.

We do not sell your personal information for monetary consideration. Where data-sharing practices are subject to specific jurisdictional requirements, we will comply with applicable rules and offer required opt-outs.

6. Data Retention and Security

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law (for example, for tax, accounting, or legal compliance purposes).

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect the information we process from loss, misuse, unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. However, no method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

If you believe your interaction with us is no longer secure, please notify us immediately using the contact details below.
