Frequently asked questions

Answers to common questions about Frontier and CenturyLink dedicated internet, business fiber, installation costs, and how our procurement support works.

Business fiber refers to fiber-based connectivity delivered to your location, which may be shared or dedicated depending on the service. Dedicated Internet Access (DIA) is a specific type of service where the full bandwidth of the circuit is reserved exclusively for your organization, with guaranteed speeds, clear SLAs, and typically symmetrical upload and download. Frontier, CenturyLink, and Lumen all offer both shared and dedicated options, and our role is to help you decide where DIA is essential and where business fiber with different characteristics may be sufficient.
Frontier, CenturyLink, and Lumen frequently run promotions that waive non-recurring installation charges for qualifying business fiber and dedicated internet orders. Eligibility depends on factors such as contract term, bandwidth, construction needs, and location. When we scope your project, we check current carrier programs and negotiate to secure free or reduced install fees wherever possible. You will see all one-time and monthly charges clearly broken out in your proposals so there are no surprises.
While we have deep experience with Frontier, CenturyLink, and Lumen dedicated internet, we operate as a carrier-neutral procurement partner. That means we can and do recommend alternative providers in markets where they offer better performance, pricing, or build timelines. Our priority is aligning connectivity with your requirements, not steering you to a specific carrier. In many rollouts, the final design includes a mix of multiple providers for resilience and cost optimization.
For most locations, we can provide preliminary Frontier, CenturyLink, and Lumen dedicated internet pricing within 24 hours once we have your site addresses and requirements. Firm pricing may take slightly longer if fiber qualification or construction review is needed. Installation timelines vary by carrier and construction scope, but typical DIA installs range from 30 to 90 days. We manage the process end-to-end and provide milestone updates so you can plan around realistic go-live dates.
We typically partner with IT for technical requirements—such as bandwidth, redundancy, routing, and demarcation details—while working with finance or procurement on commercial terms and approvals. Our proposals clearly separate technical recommendations from cost breakdowns so each stakeholder has what they need. We can join joint review calls, provide side-by-side comparisons of Frontier, CenturyLink, and Lumen options, and help build business cases that quantify uptime, performance, and total cost of ownership benefits.
